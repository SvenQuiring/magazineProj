Agustin Breccia


1-How are you coping with the online classes?
Good good, at first i didn’t find easy to get used to it because i started with blackboards but then i realised that the quality of the camera wasn’t good so i started using digital tablets, that make things easier, and then i coped very well with online classes

2-Was it difficult or not?
I didn’t find it difficult, i found it different, a challenge The most difficult part was not being able to see the students’ faces, their copybooks and the way they were working. I had to trust a lot in what they say they were doing and not in what i was able to see

3-How did students react?
On the first week of quarantine i started to organize all this stuff to start with online classes and because of the fact that i started earlier with the organization, i was one of the first teachers to start with this kind of classes. And then, other of the most difficult parts was de evaluation, i didn’t know how to evaluate students and i continue with some doubts because i don’t know how to know if they really understood a concept or they are looking it on internet or with a partner

4-What was the most difficult part of teaching online?
They reacted very well, if they didn’t lie to me of course. They all say they understood things in the online classes, i don’t know if they’re lying but i trust they aren’t.

5-How do you fell about yours students, do you feel they learn what you wanted?
I trust them, i liked the way online classes were but all students participate. I still have to evaluate if they really understood what i taught, that is my big doubt. They say that they understood all but i have my doubts and i want to make sure they understood.

6-Do you fell like you could teach as you planned ?
No, when a started with online classes i skipped some topics, it doesn't mean i’m not going to teach them, i’m going to teach them later. I have to change the topics’ order. But i’m going to teach the same topics that i was going to teach in face to face classes
