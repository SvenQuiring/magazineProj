Digital Citizenship:



What is it?:



"Digital citizenship" is a concept that is in permanent construction and that refers mainly to our behaviors and attitudes in digital spaces, and at the same time to our rights and obligations.

“Digital citizenship is a set of competencies that empowers citizens to access, retrieve, understand, evaluate and use, to create, as well as share information and media content in all formats, using various tools, in a critical way, ethical and effective in order to participate and engage in personal, professional and social activities ”. UNESCO

"Building citizenship in digital environments" is a joint effort of a system that includes the State, academia, companies, organized civil society, as well as each of us who are part of society from the different roles we occupy.

The equitable development of our society implies considering digital citizenship as a central component for the exercise of SXXI citizenship. It implies the development of skills that allow critical assumption of the information received from the media, an understanding of the social and economic impact of technology, respect for the laws; and the knowledge, mobilization and defense of their own rights in a digital environment.


Developing joint actions to promote digital citizenship:


Based on the recognition of this situation, we considered it necessary to develop joint work with different organizations and institutions related to the issue and agree on common reference frameworks on Digital Citizenship, which allow us to generate coordinated and aligned awareness strategies.

The Digital Citizenship group is made up of references from the following organizations and institutions: Unesco Uruguay, Unicef Uruguay, Codicen-Anep, Plan Ceibal, Fundación Ceibal, MEC - Directorate of Education and Technology, National Human Rights Institution, University of the República– ObservaTIC, NGO El abrojo, NGO Pensamiento Colectivo, MIEM, Universidad Tecnológica del Uruguay and Agesic.

This group has worked on the development of a series of agreements that include the types of use of digital citizenship and the skills necessary to develop them:

Safe and responsible use.
Within this dimension are the skills of:

  • Self-regulation (time on screen).
  • Ethical and empathetic behavior.
  • Know and exercise rights in the digital environment.
  • Fingerprint awareness.
  • Construction of digital identity.
  • Privacy management.
  • Risk management.
    •


Critical and thoughtful use.
Within this dimension are the skills of:

  • Ability to analyze the information received.
  • Ability to question content.
  • Understand the non-neutrality of the web.
  • Understand the operation of algorithms and their impacts.
  • Identify the interests involved.
  • Be aware of the digital ecosystem and its implications.
    •


Creative and participatory use.
Within this dimension are the skills of:

  • Ability to develop digital content.
  • Understanding of digital divides.
  • Ability to innovate with ICT.
  • Development of communication skills.
  • Use of ICT to generate personal and social transformation.
    •


In this framework, a series of didactic resources were generated that include guides, games and testimonial videos among others to address topics such as: screen time, critical spirit, fake news, coexistence, rules for the use of devices, security, trust and privacy, reflection on the terms and conditions that are required for the use of applications, the main lines to be developed in education, among others.
